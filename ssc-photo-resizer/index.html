Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the SSC photo size in KB?

For most SSC exams like CGL, CHSL, MTS and GD, the photo size is usually required between 20KB and 50KB in JPG format. Always check the official notification for exact limits.

2. What is the SSC photo size in pixels?

SSC photos are generally required in a 3.5×4.5 cm ratio, which is around 413×531 pixels. The exact pixel requirement may vary slightly depending on the exam.

3. Can I use the same photo for SSC CGL, CHSL, MTS and GD?

Yes. Most SSC exams use the same photo specifications. You can use the same recent photograph if it meets the size, format and background requirements.

4. How to resize photo for SSC online?

Upload your photo, crop it to the required ratio, and use the compression slider to bring the file size between 20KB and 50KB. Then download the SSC-ready image.

5. What format is required for SSC photo upload?

SSC requires the photograph in JPG or JPEG format. Other formats like PNG or HEIC should be converted before uploading.

6. Can I take the SSC photo using my phone?

Yes, you can take the photo with a smartphone. Make sure the background is plain, lighting is good, and your face is clearly visible. Then resize it to SSC requirements.

7. Why is my SSC photo upload getting rejected?

Photos are usually rejected due to incorrect file size, wrong dimensions, unclear face, dark background, or wrong format. Ensure the photo meets SSC size and clarity rules.

8. Is a white background required for SSC photo?

Yes. A plain white or light background is usually required for SSC photos. Avoid shadows, patterns, or colored backgrounds.

9. How to compress SSC photo to 20KB or 50KB?

Use an SSC photo compressor or resizer tool. Adjust the compression slider until the file size falls within the required 20KB to 50KB range.

10. What should I wear for the SSC photo?

Wear simple, plain clothing that contrasts with the background. Avoid caps, sunglasses, or heavy accessories. Your face should be clearly visible.
